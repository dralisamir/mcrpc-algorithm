Introduction & Core Principles

Scope. Evidence‑informed options for metastatic castration‑resistant prostate cancer (mCRPC), summarized for rapid clinical reference and teaching.

  • Continue ADT indefinitely. Add palliative/supportive care early; maintain bone health (denosumab or zoledronic acid + Ca/Vit‑D).
  • Monitoring. Use CT chest/abdomen/pelvis + Tc‑99 bone scan q3–6 months or earlier with clinical change. PSMA‑PET is primarily to qualify patients for ¹⁷⁷Lu‑PSMA‑617; it is not recommended for routine response assessment.
  • PSA kinetics (PSADT/velocity). In mCRPC, PSA kinetics are not validated to determine therapy selection; avoid PSA‑only progression calls without clinical/radiographic corroboration (PCWG3‑consistent).
Evidence basis: ASCO 2025 guideline recommendations on monitoring and response assessment.

Quick Definitions

HRR genes & BRCA

HRR (homologous recombination repair) genes include BRCA1/2, PALB2, ATM, CHEK2, CDK12, among others. BRCA1/2 alterations predict marked benefit from PARPi strategies (often with ARPI in ARPI‑naïve mCRPC).

MSI‑H/dMMR

MSI‑H/dMMR tumors can respond to pembrolizumab across histologies; in prostate cancer, activity is seen in a small subset following prior lines.

Monitoring & Response Assessment

What to order and when
  • Labs: PSA, testosterone (to confirm castrate levels), CBC, CMP; do not escalate therapy for PSA‑only rise without clinical or imaging evidence.
  • Imaging: CT‑CAP + bone scan every 3–6 months or earlier if clinically indicated.
  • PSMA‑PET: primarily for selecting ¹⁷⁷Lu‑PSMA‑617 candidates; not recommended as a routine response tool.

Source framework: ASCO Guideline Update 2025; pivotal trials as linked above. This educational tool does not substitute for clinical judgment.
