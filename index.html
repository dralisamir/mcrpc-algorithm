mCRPC Clinical Handbook (ASCO 2025)

Evidence-informed systemic therapy recommendations with PFS/OS benefits and citations

Introduction

This clinical handbook summarizes the 2025 ASCO guideline update for metastatic castration-resistant prostate cancer (mCRPC), integrating:

  • Therapeutic selection based on prior castration-sensitive phase (CSPC) exposure
  • HRR and MSI/MMR biomarkers
  • Clinical context (bone-only disease, indolent disease, oligoprogression)
  • PFS/OS benefits with trial citations

Key Definitions

  • mCRPC — Progression despite castrate testosterone (<50 ng/dL), by radiographic or biochemical criteria.
  • HRR genes — BRCA1/2, PALB2, ATM, CHEK2, CDK12, etc.
  • MSI-H/dMMR — Predictive for immune checkpoint response.
  • Oligoprogression — Limited site progression (≤3 lesions) while overall disease is controlled.

Clinical Principles

  • Continue **ADT indefinitely**.
  • Initiate **early palliative/supportive care**.
  • Use **denosumab or zoledronic acid** for bone metastases.
  • Avoid treatment changes for **PSA-only progression** without clinical/radiographic correlation.
  • Imaging: **CT CAP + bone scan q3–6 months**.
  • Use **PSMA PET** primarily to determine suitability for **¹⁷⁷Lu‑PSMA‑617**.

1. Patients Previously Treated With ADT Alone

A. BRCA1/2 Positive → PARPi + ARPI

PARPi ARPI

Preferred regimens: olaparib + abiraterone; niraparib + abiraterone; talazoparib + enzalutamide.

Evidence Summary
  • PFS: HR 0.28 (PROpel, TALAPRO‑2, MAGNITUDE BRCA subset)
  • OS: HR 0.47 (same pooled BRCA subset)
  • Strongest benefit in BRCA1/2 vs other HRR
Citations: PROpel, TALAPRO‑2, MAGNITUDE

B. Non-BRCA HRR → Talazoparib + Enzalutamide

PARPi ARPI
  • PFS: HR 0.71 (TALAPRO‑2 non‑BRCA subset)
  • OS: Not statistically significant
Citation: TALAPRO‑2

C. HRR-Negative → Abiraterone / Enzalutamide / Docetaxel

ARPI Taxane
  • Abiraterone: PFS HR 0.52, OS HR 0.81 (COU‑AA‑302)
  • Enzalutamide: PFS HR 0.32, OS HR 0.77 (PREVAIL)
  • Docetaxel: OS HR 0.79 (TAX‑327)

2. Patients Previously Treated With ADT + ARPI

A. BRCA1/2 Positive → Olaparib Monotherapy

PARPi
  • PFS: HR 0.22 (PROfound BRCA subset)
  • OS: HR 0.63
Citations: PROfound, TRITON‑3

B. HRR– or Non-BRCA HRR → Docetaxel (Avoid ARPI→ARPI)

Taxane
  • CARD trial: Cabazitaxel superior to abiraterone→enzalutamide or vice versa
  • PFS: 8.0 vs 3.7 months (HR 0.54)
  • OS: 13.6 vs 11.0 months (HR 0.64)

3. Patients Previously Treated With ADT + Docetaxel

A. HRR-positive → PARPi + ARPI if ARPI-naïve

PARPi ARPI
  • Same benefits as BRCA1/2 subset in PROpel/TALAPRO‑2/MAGNITUDE

B. HRR-negative → Abiraterone / Enzalutamide / Cabazitaxel

ARPI Taxane
  • Abiraterone post-docetaxel: PFS HR 0.66, OS HR 0.74 (COU‑AA‑301)
  • Enzalutamide post-docetaxel: OS HR 0.63 (AFFIRM)
  • Cabazitaxel: OS HR 0.70 (TROPIC)

4. Patients Previously Treated With ADT + ARPI + Docetaxel (Triplet)

A. PSMA-positive → ¹⁷⁷Lu‑PSMA‑617

Radiopharmaceutical
  • PFS: HR 0.40 (VISION)
  • OS: HR 0.62
Citation: VISION

B. HRR-positive → Olaparib Monotherapy

PARPi
  • PFS: HR 0.49 (PROfound HRR group)

C. Symptomatic Bone-only → Radium‑223

Radiopharmaceutical
  • OS: HR 0.70 (ALSYMPCA)

5. Special Clinical Situations

Indolent, Minimally Symptomatic → Sipuleucel‑T

Cellular therapy
  • OS benefit: HR 0.73
  • No expected PSA or radiographic response
Citations: IMPACT, D9901, D9902A

MSI-H/dMMR → Pembrolizumab

Immunotherapy
  • ORR: ~40% across histology (KEYNOTE‑158)
  • Modest prostate-specific sample size

Oligoprogression → Metastasis-Directed Therapy (SBRT/Surgery)

MDT
  • Improved PFS when added to abiraterone in oligomet mCRPC
Citation: ARTO trial

Neuroendocrine / Small-cell Variant → Platinum-based Therapy

Platinum Taxane
  • Carboplatin/cisplatin + etoposide (SCLC-based paradigm)
  • Carboplatin + cabazitaxel for AVPC phenotype
